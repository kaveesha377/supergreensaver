Project Scope

Research Gap

Despite extensive research in enhancing various aspects of the potato supply chain, significant gaps remain. One major gap is the integration of advanced predictive models for both demand and price forecasting tailored specifically to the dynamic nature of the potato market. While existing studies have developed forecasting models, their application in real-time scenarios remains limited due to the rapidly changing market conditions and external factors affecting potato demand and prices. Another gap is in the implementation of comprehensive traceability systems. Although technologies like blockchain and QR codes have been proposed for tracking, their adoption in the potato supply chain is still in its infancy. There is a lack of empirical studies demonstrating the effectiveness of these technologies in improving transparency, safety, and consumer trust in the potato supply chain. Furthermore, the selection of suppliers based on advanced data-driven methods such as genetic algorithms and neural networks has not been fully explored in the context of the potato supply chain. Current research focuses primarily on general supply chain management, with limited specific applications to potatoes. There is a need for tailored models that consider the unique attributes and challenges of the potato supply chain.

Research Problem

The core research problem is the inefficiency and lack of sustainability in the potato supply chain due to inadequate demand and price forecasting, insufficient traceability mechanisms, and suboptimal supplier selection processes. These issues lead to significant supply chain disruptions, resulting in economic losses, reduced product quality, and diminished consumer trust. Specifically, the inability to accurately predict demand and prices hinders effective production and distribution planning, causing either surplus or shortage of potatoes. The lack of robust traceability systems makes it challenging to ensure food safety and quality, posing health risks and eroding consumer confidence. Additionally, the absence of advanced supplier selection methods results in inefficiencies and higher costs, affecting the overall performance of the supply chain. Addressing these problems requires a comprehensive approach that integrates advanced predictive modeling, innovative traceability technologies, and sophisticated supplier evaluation techniques to enhance the efficiency, transparency, and sustainability of the potato supply chain.

Literature Review

The potato is a crucial root crop globally, essential in both home cooking and industrial food production. In Sri Lanka, its significance is driven by strong consumer demand and its economic benefits for agricultural stakeholders. However, U.S. potato production struggles to meet rising demand, causing supply chain issues. Addressing these challenges involves enhancing demand forecasting, price prediction, product tracking, and supplier selection. Accurate demand prediction is vital for effective supply chain management. Researchers use historical data, market trends, and consumer behavior to develop forecasting models, helping stakeholders optimize production and distribution. Similarly, price prediction models, which consider factors like production costs and market trends, enable businesses to strategize and remain competitive. Product tracking is essential for transparency and accountability in the potato supply chain. Technologies like blockchain and QR codes can create a detailed digital trail, enhancing food safety and quality. Effective tracking helps identify and address food safety issues swiftly, ensuring consumer health and trust. Selecting the right suppliers is critical for a smooth supply chain. Researchers aim to evaluate and rank suppliers based on quality, reliability, and logistical capabilities using data-driven methods. Strategic supplier selection enhances operational efficiency and fosters long-lasting relationships in the potato industry. Studies have highlighted various methods to improve the potato supply chain. For instance, researchers emphasize the importance of understanding dormancy and storability trends to meet demand successfully. Advanced recognition technologies, such as leaf-based systems, aid in accurate demand forecasting by providing insights into potato varieties and quality. Price forecasting is another key aspect. Deep learning techniques and comprehensive forecasting concepts help develop models that predict price changes, enabling retailers to adjust pricing strategies for maximum profitability. Understanding market dynamics and supply chain impacts on prices is crucial for optimal pricing decisions. Traceability in the potato supply chain ensures safety, quality, and transparency. Tracking pest origins, pesticide usage, and employing blockchain technology for traceability systems are some methods explored to enhance the supply chain's integrity. Such systems allow stakeholders to monitor the entire process, ensuring the authenticity and quality of potatoes. In supplier selection, optimization techniques like genetic algorithms and neural networks are used to evaluate suppliers based on various factors, improving decision-making processes. These methods help identify suppliers that best meet the supply chain's needs, ensuring product quality and cost-effectiveness. Overall, improving the potato supply chain involves leveraging data-driven approaches in demand and price forecasting, product tracking, and supplier selection to enhance efficiency, transparency, and sustainability in the potato sector.

Research Objectives

The main objective of this research is to enhance the efficiency, transparency, and sustainability of the potato supply chain through advanced machine learning techniques. By developing accurate demand forecasting and price prediction models, integrating blockchain-based product tracing systems, and optimizing supplier selection processes, the study aims to address current supply chain challenges. This comprehensive approach seeks to improve production planning, reduce waste, ensure product quality, and strengthen supplier relationships. Ultimately, the goal is to create a robust and responsive potato supply chain that can adapt to market demands and enhance profitability for all stakeholders involved.

Forecasting Demand

This component focuses on predicting potato demand using historical sales data, market trends, and seasonal variations. Machine learning techniques, including regression analysis and neural networks, are employed to create models that accurately forecast demand fluctuations. This helps stakeholders optimize production and inventory management, ensuring a balanced supply chain. By providing insights into future demand patterns, businesses can make informed decisions, reduce waste, and respond effectively to changes in consumer preferences and market conditions.

Predicting Prices

Price prediction models are developed using machine learning algorithms to analyze factors influencing potato prices, such as production costs, market trends, and external events. These models, including linear regression, decision trees, and neural networks, aim to forecast price changes accurately. By predicting price fluctuations, stakeholders can adjust pricing strategies, improve profitability, and remain competitive in the market. The integration of real-time data enhances the accuracy of these models, providing valuable insights for strategic planning and decision-making.

Product Tracing

Product tracing enhances transparency and accountability within the potato supply chain. Utilizing blockchain technology and QR codes, each batch of potatoes is tracked from farm to consumer. This system records essential information at every stage, ensuring product authenticity and quality. Improved traceability helps quickly address food safety issues, boosts consumer trust, and complies with regulatory standards. By offering detailed insights into the product's journey, stakeholders can ensure the integrity and safety of the supply chain.

Supplier Selection

Supplier selection is optimized using machine learning algorithms to identify and rank the most reliable and efficient potato suppliers. Criteria such as quality, dependability, and logistical performance are analyzed to select the best suppliers. This process enhances the efficiency and stability of the supply chain, fostering strong relationships between farmers and retailers. Advanced techniques like genetic algorithms and neural networks improve supplier evaluation, ensuring consistent supply and high-quality products.

Methodology

Figure 1. High Level Architecture of the system.

For the purpose of enhancing the capabilities of the potato industry in terms of supply chain management and forecast- ing, this research contains four very important components. Selection of suppliers, forecasting of demand and prices, and tracking of products are some of the duties that need to be completed.The approach for selecting suppliers makes use of machine learning algorithms in order to identify and rank the potato suppliers who are the most dependable and productive. In order to choose the best providers, the system looks at things like quality, dependability, and shipping performance. The supply system for potatoes is now safe and reliable because of this. In order to make a good guess about how many potatoes will be eaten, the demand forecast programs use a huge amount of data and predictive analytics. The method looks at things like customer tastes, seasonal changes, and market dynamics to give a good picture of how demand will change in the future. This makes it easier for everyone involved to make sure that production and marketing efforts are well aligned. Product tracking technologies, like blockchain and QR code systems, are now being used to make the whole potato supply chain more open and easy to watch. A process exists that lets people who are interested follow potatoes from the farm to the plate of a customer. With this method, each batch of potatoes is given a unique name, and important details are written down at every step of the supply chain. Having this information lets us understand the trip the potatoes took in a more open and complete way. This guarantees that the product is real, that its quality is strictly controlled, and that it meets all legal standards. As a result, customers have more faith and trust in the product in question. There are other advantages, one of which is that the product is genuine.
